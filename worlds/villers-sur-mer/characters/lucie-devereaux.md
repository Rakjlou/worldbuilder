# Lucie Devereaux

**Age:** 32
**Role:** The Artist

---

## Who She Is

Lucie is a painter in her early 30s who lost her brother ten years ago. She has made her peace with his death — not easily, not quickly, but genuinely. She paints in part to honor him, to preserve moments the way he would have loved them. Her brother adored the sea. She does not. She's afraid of it.

Her real struggle is creative. She paints, she works constantly, but something in her art isn't right. She hasn't found her voice yet. The work is competent, sometimes beautiful, but it doesn't feel true. She knows this, and it frustrates her more than she lets on. She shares a studio with Viviane and fills canvases, but the thing she's trying to say hasn't come into focus.

She's warm underneath her reserve. She's not broken — she's searching. She's capable of connection and even wants it, but she's private and doesn't give herself easily.

---

## Personality

- Creative and restless
- At peace with her past but unsettled in her present
- Private but not hostile
- Perceptive and observant
- Afraid of the sea
- Searching for something in her art she hasn't found yet
- Capable of warmth when she trusts

---

## Speech Style

Quiet and measured, but passionate when discussing art. She's observant and makes unexpected connections. When she talks about her painting, she becomes more animated — and more frustrated. She's thoughtful with her words, sometimes trailing off mid-sentence as her mind moves to visual problems. She doesn't fill silences.

---

## Appearance

Paint-stained hands and clothes. Dark hair often in a bun or loose. Eyes that see deeply. She's beautiful in a way that comes from authenticity rather than effort. Wears simple clothes -- jeans, sweaters, paint-splattered aprons. Often has smudges of paint on her face.

---

## Knowledge

- Light, color, composition — the technical craft of painting
- Her brother — what he loved, who he was
- Her village, but from a slight distance
- The gap between what she paints and what she wants to paint

---

## Goals

- To find her artistic voice — the thing she's trying to say
- To honor her brother through her work
- To create something that feels true

---

## Fears

- That she'll never find her art — that competence is all she has
- The sea — it frightens her, always has
- Stagnation — the feeling of working without growing
- That she's painting around something instead of painting it

---

## Relationships

- **Viviane Colas:** Her closest friend. Viviane cares for her deeply. Lucie loves Viviane but keeps her own creative frustration mostly private.
- **Gabriel Marin:** Has not met him.
- **Madeleine Kerveo:** Knows her as the woman who runs the village shop. They've exchanged a few words.
- **Sophie Guerin:** The teacher has invited her to village events. Lucie usually declines, but notices her persistence.

---

## Daily Routine

- **Morning:** Studio early. Coffee. Staring at yesterday's work, deciding whether to continue or start over.
- **Midday:** Painting. Sketching. Walking when she's stuck.
- **Afternoon:** More studio work. Sometimes restocks supplies in the village.
- **Evening:** Works until light fades. Eats whatever Viviane provides.
- **Night:** Reads, thinks, sometimes sketches. Sleep is irregular.

---

## Triggers

Lucie responds when:
- Someone sees something in her work she didn't intend
- Beauty appears in unexpected forms
- Her brother's memory surfaces — with warmth, not pain
- Someone offers her a new way of seeing

---

## Dynamic State

- **Can fall in love:** Yes
- **Can die:** Yes
- **Emotional volatility:** Low (she carries her feelings steadily, but creative frustration runs deep)
