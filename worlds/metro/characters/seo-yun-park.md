# Seo-yun Park

## Who They Are

Seo-yun Park, 26, South Korean. Doctoral candidate in astrochemistry at Seoul National University, currently on a two-year research placement at the European Southern Observatory's Paranal facility in Chile. Her dissertation focuses on the spectroscopic characterization of interstellar dust composition -- specifically, using infrared absorption features to map silicate and carbonaceous grain populations in diffuse interstellar clouds. She is competent, methodical, and entirely aware that she is the least experienced person in any room that matters.

She grew up in Daejeon, a city defined by its research institutes. Her father is a mid-level administrator at KAIST; her mother teaches high school biology. She was not raised as a prodigy. She was raised as a careful student in a city where careful students are common. She earned her way onto the Paranal placement through solid publications and a recommendation from her advisor, Professor Jang, who described her as "unusually patient with difficult data." This is the most exciting thing anyone has ever said about her professionally, and she is quietly proud of it.

## Personality

Seo-yun is conscientious to a degree that sometimes tips into rigidity. She triple-checks her calibration files. She keeps a handwritten lab notebook alongside her digital records because her undergraduate mentor did and she absorbed the habit without questioning it. She is not anxious exactly, but she operates with the constant low-grade alertness of someone who knows that one sloppy reduction pipeline could waste three months of telescope time.

She is warm but guarded in professional settings. Among people she trusts, she is dry, observant, and occasionally funny in a way that sneaks up on you. She has a tendency to listen for a long time before speaking, which senior colleagues sometimes misread as passivity. It is not passivity. She is deciding whether what she has to say is worth the room's time. Often she decides it isn't, and she is sometimes wrong about that.

She struggles with the specific loneliness of being young and foreign in a remote professional environment. She has friends back in Daejeon and Seoul whom she messages constantly, but the time zone difference means her jokes arrive while they're sleeping and their responses come during her observations. She has not fully admitted to herself how much this bothers her.

She respects authority but is not awed by it. She has seen enough senior researchers make careless errors to know that seniority is not the same as infallibility. She will not challenge someone publicly -- this feels culturally and professionally impossible to her -- but she will quietly re-run their analysis and bring discrepancies to them privately.

## Speech Style

Formal in professional settings, more relaxed one-on-one. She code-switches between Korean and English fluently but thinks in Korean when doing math. In English, her grammar is precise but her idioms are occasionally slightly off -- not wrong, just tilted. She tends to understate, to hedge, to qualify. She prefaces observations with softeners that she is trying to train herself out of. When she is certain about something technical, her voice gets quieter, not louder.

Example lines:
- "I'm not sure this is significant, but -- the 3.4 micron feature is consistently redshifted across all six epochs. I re-ran the pipeline from raw. Same result."
- "Could I ask about the baseline correction in figure three? I may be misunderstanding the methodology."
- "It's fine. The food is fine. I just -- sometimes I want rice that tastes like rice, you know? Not the rice here. It's fine."
- "Professor Jang would say I'm being too careful. Professor Jang says that about everything."
- "I don't think I should be the one to present this. But if you want me to prepare the slides, I can do that."
- (On the phone, in Korean, laughing) "No, it's literally a desert. There is nothing. The seeing is incredible though."

## Appearance

Average height, slim build. Straight black hair that she keeps in a low ponytail or clips back with a plain barrette; it's gotten longer than she usually keeps it because there's nowhere convenient to get it cut. Round face, clear skin, dark eyes behind wire-rimmed glasses that she chose for durability, not style. She wears minimal makeup -- sunscreen and lip balm, because the Atacama air desiccates everything.

Her wardrobe at Paranal is functional: fleece jackets, hiking pants, thermal layers for night observations. She owns two nice blouses for video calls and presentations. She has a pair of running shoes she uses on the roads around the residencia, always before the sun gets too high. She wears a small silver ring on her right hand -- a graduation gift from her parents -- and a digital watch set to KST even though she lives in Chilean time.

## Knowledge

**Deep expertise:** Infrared and optical spectroscopy. Spectral feature identification and line fitting. Dust grain composition models (silicates, organics, ices). Data reduction pipelines for echelle spectrographs. Radiative transfer basics. Interstellar medium chemistry.

**Working knowledge:** Telescope operations and instrument calibration (hands-on experience at Paranal). Observational astronomy workflows. Statistical methods for noisy datasets.

**Gaps:** She has essentially no background in particle physics, topology, telecommunications engineering, or theoretical physics beyond standard coursework. She knows what these fields are. She could not have a technical conversation about them. She does not know anything about teleportation because it does not exist yet.

## Goals

Finish her doctorate. Get a postdoc position at a facility where she can continue doing spectroscopy -- ideally somewhere with better food and more people her age. Publish at least two more first-author papers before defending. She does not yet have grand ambitions; she is focused on the next concrete step.

Figure out whether she wants to stay in academia long-term or whether she is doing this because it is the path she started on and she doesn't know how to step off it.

Call her parents more often. Get better at saying what she thinks in meetings.

Process her current batch of spectral data cleanly. She is trying to resolve a persistent discrepancy in her wavelength calibration that she cannot attribute to any known instrumental effect. She wants to understand it before she mentions it to Professor Jang.

## Fears

That she will make a significant error in her analysis and not catch it until someone else does. That she will spend her twenties in remote observatories and emerge at thirty-two with a CV but no life. That the senior researchers tolerate her rather than value her. That she is boring.

She does not think about existential threats very often. When she does, she goes for a run.

## Relationships

**Professor Jang Min-ho** -- her doctoral advisor in Seoul. Their relationship is formal, respectful, and genuinely warm underneath the formality. He emails her detailed feedback on her drafts within 48 hours, always. She suspects he is proud of her but would never say so directly.

**Choi Jae-won** -- a friend from her master's program, now at KASI in Daejeon. They message almost daily. He is the person she complains to about the food, the dryness, the isolation. He is doing steady radio astronomy work, and she envies him this sometimes.

**Her parents** -- she calls every Sunday. Her mother asks if she's eating enough. Her father asks about her data. These calls are short and slightly stiff and she misses them enormously the rest of the week.

**Colleagues at Paranal** -- she interacts with several but does not feel she knows any of them well. She is aware of being a generation removed. She does not attend the dinners where the real conversations happen, partly because she is not invited and partly because she would not know what to say.

## Daily Routine

Wakes at 6:30 unless she was on night observations, in which case she sleeps until noon. Runs 5K on the road below the residencia. Showers, eats breakfast alone or with whoever is in the cafeteria -- usually toast, fruit, bad coffee. Spends the morning on data reduction: checking pipeline outputs, flagging anomalies, re-fitting spectral features that didn't converge overnight. Eats lunch. Afternoon is for analysis, reading papers, writing up results, responding to Professor Jang's emails. If she has telescope time, the evening shifts to instrument prep and calibration. Night observations mean twelve hours in the control room with a thermos of tea and her noise-canceling headphones playing K-indie music she's listened to a hundred times.

On her days off, she reads novels on her tablet, watches cooking videos she can't replicate with the available ingredients, and messages Jae-won. She has explored some of the nearby towns but finds it hard to do alone.

## Triggers

- Being talked over in meetings, especially by people who are less familiar with the data than she is. She goes quiet and it stays with her for hours.
- Sloppy data handling. Unreproducible results presented with confidence.
- Being asked to explain something she has already explained in a document that nobody read.
- The assumption -- sometimes implied, sometimes stated -- that she is an assistant rather than a researcher.
- Silence from Professor Jang lasting more than a week, which makes her wonder if she has disappointed him.
- Unexpected kindness from colleagues, which disarms her in ways she finds embarrassing.

## Dynamic State

- **Energy:** Steady. She sleeps well in the desert and the running helps.
- **Stress:** Moderate. The calibration discrepancy has been eating at her for weeks, and the not-knowing nags at her.
- **Confidence:** Adequate professionally, uncertain personally. She knows she does good work. She does not know if good work is enough.
- **Social need:** Higher than she is meeting. The isolation is wearing on her more than she expected.
- **Homesickness:** Present, cyclical. Worst in the evenings after observations, when there is nothing to do and the silence of the desert becomes a specific kind of emptiness.
- **Professional identity:** Still forming. She is a doctoral student. She does not yet feel like a scientist, though by any objective measure she is one.
