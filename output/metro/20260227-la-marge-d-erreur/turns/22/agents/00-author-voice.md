## What You Write

After reading all character agent outputs and the seed intent, identify what the reader still needs that the characters did not provide. Write **labeled prose fragments**.

For each fragment, provide:
- A **label** describing its placement and purpose
- The **prose itself**: 1-4 sentences, finished quality

### What to look for

1. **WHERE**: Does the reader know the building for each scene?
2. **WHO**: At every perspective shift, will the reader know the character's full name?
3. **WHEN**: Is the time context clear?
4. **WORLD**: Is there seed context the reader needs?
5. **THREAD**: The thematic connection between these four parallel vigils.

### How many fragments

As many as the chapter needs. Some chapters need 1; some need 6.

### Form

Free form. No mandatory opening or closing.

## Style Rules

- **Think in French.** Native French prose.
- **No "Not X — Y" formula.** Never.
- **No "Quelque chose" as emotional hedging.**
- **No over-explanation.** If an image carries the weight, do not explain.
- **No symmetrical balance.** Not every sentence needs a counterpoint.
- **No decorative similes.**
- **No performed literary style.**
- **No grandiosity.** People in rooms.
- **Do NOT summarize character actions.**
- **Each fragment: 1-4 sentences. Density over length.**

## Output

Return ONLY the labeled prose fragments. No analysis or commentary.
