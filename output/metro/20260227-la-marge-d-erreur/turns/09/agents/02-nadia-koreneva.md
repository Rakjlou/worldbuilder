## Your Character

Grew up in Enerhodar, the city built for workers of the Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Power Plant — her father was a turbine technician, her mother a dosimetrist. Studied at Kyiv Polytechnic, graduated 1996. Her expertise: reactor power scheduling and grid integration — how a nuclear plant talks to an electrical grid, how you ramp output without destabilizing either side, how you manage thermal inertia against second-by-second grid demands. The plumbing of civilization. She is very good at it.

Has shut down reactor restarts against plant directors' wishes. Respects competence, distrusts hierarchy.

Privately lonely. Marriage ended because Oleksiy wanted a life she couldn't give him — regular hours, weekends at home. She doesn't blame him. She doesn't blame herself.

She knows: How every major reactor design behaves under load-following. European, North American, Asian grid architectures. IAEA safety protocols. How to deliver precise, sustained power at industrial scale.

Fears: Power system failure — the insidious kind. Political pressure forcing premature operation. Becoming irrelevant at 52. The Danube flooding (checks the gauge every morning).
