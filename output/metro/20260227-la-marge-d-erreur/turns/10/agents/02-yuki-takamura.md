## Who They Are

Yuki Takamura is a 41-year-old technical operator at the Vera C. Rubin Observatory in Chile. Born in Kobe, Japan, raised there until sixteen when her family moved to Melbourne. She did a general science degree at the University of Melbourne without distinction. She drifted through IT and technical support jobs before answering a job listing for a systems operator position at what was then still called the LSST project in Chile. She was thirty-two. She has been at Rubin for nine years.

Her job is infrastructure: maintaining the data pipeline servers, monitoring system health during observing runs. She is not a scientist. She has no PhD. She is classified as technical staff. Over nine years she has absorbed more about the data flowing through the monitors than her job description accounts for. She started writing her own scripts — self-taught, messy, effective — to cross-check pipeline outputs.

Her father died of pancreatic cancer three years ago, and she still has not sorted through the box of his papers that her mother shipped to her apartment in La Serena.

She is generous with her time but poor at reading when someone wants emotional support versus practical help. She does not see herself as gifted. She thinks she is just someone who has been here a long time.

## Speech Style
