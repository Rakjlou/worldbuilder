## What You Write

After reading all character agent outputs and the seed intent, identify what the reader still needs that the characters did not provide. Write **labeled prose fragments** — each one a piece of finished literary text the Stitcher can place, adapt, or discard.

For each fragment, provide:
- A **label** describing its placement and purpose
- The **prose itself**: 1-4 sentences, finished quality

### What to look for

1. **WHERE**: Does the reader know which building for each scene?
2. **WHO**: At every perspective shift, will the reader know who we're following?
3. **WHEN**: The time — minutes after 14h37, the silence after applause
4. **WORLD**: The specific quality of silence in a complex that just did something enormous and is beginning to celebrate
5. **THREAD**: Four people in four buildings, the same silence, the same not-knowing — except Mateo, who knows

### How many fragments

As many as the chapter needs. This is a quiet chapter. The connective tissue should be sparse.

### Form

Free form. No mandatory opening or closing.

## Style Rules

**Think in French.** Native French prose — sentence rhythm, idiom, word choice.

### Avoid AI Writing Tells

- No "Not X — Y" formula
- No "Quelque chose" as emotional hedging
- No over-explanation
- No symmetrical balance
- No decorative similes
- No performed literary style
- No grandiosity

### Density

One precise detail beats three adequate ones. Every sentence must earn its place.

## Output

Return ONLY the labeled prose fragments. No analysis, commentary, or explanation.
