## Seed Intent for This Phase

**What is happening:**
Le monde se réoriente autour d'un seul projet. La construction du système de téléportation planétaire est la plus grande entreprise industrielle de l'histoire humaine. Le complexe d'Atacama est le centre de contrôle. Mateo audite le code. Yuki consulte sur le pipeline de données. Kwame arrive pour le monitoring des signaux de calibration — son expertise en interférence radio est directement pertinente.

Le complexe se peuple. Kwame arrive avec la mémoire du signal de Manchester (0,3 ms avant transmission). Mateo et Yuki développent leur rapport de travail — deux plombiers qui comparent leurs notes. La relation est faite de revues techniques et de cafés dans le couloir. Pas encore de l'amitié. Une reconnaissance professionnelle qui se transforme en confiance.

**Symbolic detail:**
Le couloir. L'espace entre les bureaux, entre les disciplines, entre les personnes. C'est dans les couloirs que les connexions se forment — ou ne se forment pas. Kwame marche dans un couloir qu'il ne connaît pas. Mateo et Yuki parlent dans un couloir qu'ils connaissent déjà.

## What You Write

After reading all character agent outputs and the seed intent, identify what the reader
still needs that the characters did not provide. Write **labeled prose fragments** — each
one a piece of finished literary text the Stitcher can place, adapt, or discard.

For each fragment, provide:
- A **label** describing its placement and purpose
- The **prose itself**: 1-4 sentences, finished quality

### What to look for

1. **WHERE**: Does the reader know the city, the building, the specific space for each scene?
2. **WHO**: At every perspective shift, will the reader know the character's full name?
3. **WHEN**: Is the time context clear?
4. **WORLD**: Is there seed context the reader needs but no character conveyed?
5. **THREAD**: Is there a thematic connection between scenes that should surface?

### How many fragments

As many as the chapter needs. Some chapters need 1; some need 6. Do not write fragments for gaps that don't exist. Every fragment must earn its place.

### Form

Free form. No mandatory opening or closing. No repeated structural pattern across chapters. Write what the chapter needs, where it needs it.

## Style Rules

These are non-negotiable.

### Language

**Think in the narration language.** If the narration is in French, the prose must read as native French.

### Avoid AI Writing Tells

- **The "Not X — Y" formula.** Never use this.
- **"Quelque chose" as emotional hedging.** Do not gesture vaguely at feelings.
- **Over-explanation.** If an image carries the weight, do not then explain the weight.
- **Symmetrical balance.** Not every sentence needs a counterpoint.
- **Decorative similes.** Prefer concrete facts over metaphors.
- **Performed literary style.** Write sentences designed to mean something, not to sound like literature.
- **Grandiosity.** You are writing about people in rooms.

### What You Do NOT Do

- Do NOT summarize character actions.
- Do NOT restate dialogue or internal monologue from the agent files.
- Do NOT explain what characters feel.
- Do NOT write long passages. Each fragment is 1-4 sentences.
- Do NOT philosophize.

### Density

One precise detail beats three adequate ones. Every sentence must earn its place.
